Silicon in Computer Chips By Hao What is a Semiconductor? Semiconductors are materials with tune-able electrical conductivity. Though unusable in its natural form, processing allows it to become a functional device tuned for specific needs. What is Silicon? Silicon is one of the metalloids, which exhibits metal-like characteristics while not being a metal. Being not as good as conductors at conducting electricity and only conducting a small amount of electricity, it is thus classified as a semiconductor. What is Doping? The process of mixing silicon (in this case) with other elements and making the element impure is called doping. Since silicon atoms have 4 valence shell electrons, it also bonds with 4 other silicon atoms each, creating a lattice shape. There are two types of impurities in a silicon crystal - N-Type and P-type. In N-Type semiconductors, elements like phosphorus (with 5 valence shell electrons) can be added to create bonds with 4 other silicon atoms, with one extra electron left to move around, which increases the silicon's overall conductivity significantly, with the charge carriers being the electrons. In P-Type semiconductors, electrons with less than 3 valence shell electrons, such as boron is added to the silicon, it will bond with only 3 other silicon atoms, resulting in a "hole" between them, which will want to accept electrons into them to create more bonds, with the "holes" being the charge carriers. Why is Silicon a Good Fit For Use in Transistors? While chips require electricity to run, it requires the ability to let electricity only "pass" through certain transistors, thus not overloading the chip with instructions. Silicon also is one of the most common elements and operates well at a wide range of temperatures. Its conductivity can be changed by increasing temperature, as well as through doping it with other elements. The P-Type and N-Type silicon can be put together into PNP or NPN transistors. When the start and end terminals are connected to a power source, no electrons flow, but when a separate small current is applied to the middle semiconductor, a large current will flow from one end of the transistor to the other. By applying the current to the middle semiconductor on or off to control when current flows through a semiconductor, logic gates can be created. These logic gates are the building blocks of processing chips. Thank You!
